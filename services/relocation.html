Kampala Today

A modern capital city with a growing expatriate community

Kampala is East Africa's most underrated destination for expatriates. Kampala's seven hills host a diverse, fast-growing expat community of diplomats, NGO workers, business executives, investors and returning Ugandan diaspora. The city offers reliable international schools, excellent private healthcare, a vibrant dining scene and a warm, welcoming culture.

The practical steps from finding the right house in a safe neighbourhood, opening a bank account as a foreigner, getting children into school can be daunting without local knowledge. We've navigated this system for clients since 2022. Your relocation becomes a project, and we deliver it.

Where to Live

Kampala's prime residential neighbourhoods

Kololo
Most Prestigious
USD 1,500 – 5,000 / month

Kampala's premier address. Home to most embassies, diplomats and senior executives. Elevated position, excellent security, mature gardens. Closest area to most international offices on Nakasero Road.

Nakasero
Business District
USD 1,000 – 3,000 / month

Kampala's business and government heart. Walking distance to key ministries, major banks and the Kampala Serena Hotel. High demand from NGO headquarters and corporate tenants.

Bugolobi
Family Favourite
USD 700 – 2,800 / month

Quiet, leafy and family-oriented. Close to the International Hospital of Kampala (IHK) and several international schools. Popular with mid-level executives and NGO staff.

Ntinda
Best Value
USD 500 – 1,200 / month

Kampala's most convenient suburb. Well-connected, with excellent shopping (Ntinda Complex), restaurants and services. Growing expat community and good security posture.

Muyenga
Lake Views
USD 800 – 2,000 / month

Set on a hill above Lake Victoria. Known for large compound properties and spectacular views. A 20-minute drive to the city centre. Preferred by senior expatriates seeking space and calm.

Lubowa / Buziga
Gated Communities
USD 1,200 – 3,500 / month

Kampala's newest executive suburbs feature gated estates with 24-hour security, backup power and modern amenities. Ideal for families requiring high security and community living.

What's Included

Our full relocation package

Housing Search & Negotiation

We shortlist properties matching your requirements, arrange viewings and negotiate lease terms on your behalf. We know Kampala's rental market and which landlords have reliable generators and security.

School Applications

We guide curriculum selection, compile application documents, arrange school visits and liaise with admissions offices. For competitive schools, we advise on timeline and waitlist strategy.

Bank Account Setup

We introduce you to our relationship managers at Stanbic Bank, Standard Chartered and DFCU. Foreign nationals require a work permit and introductory letter, we provide both as part of the relocation package.

Healthcare Registration

Introductions to Nakasero Hospital and International Hospital of Kampala (IHK), both offering international-standard care. We advise on international health insurance options accepted in Uganda.

Household Staff Recruitment

Sourcing, interviewing and reference-checking housekeepers, cooks, guards and drivers. We handle employment contracts and advise on statutory deductions (NSSF, PAYE for domestic workers).

Orientation & Area Familiarisation

A half-day city orientation covering supermarkets (Carrefour, Shoprite, Capital Shoppers), markets, essential services, Kampala geography and cultural etiquette. Delivered on arrival day.

Our Process

How we move you to Kampala

01

Needs Assessment

We interview you on family composition, lifestyle priorities, commute requirements, school curriculum preferences and budget. This informs a tailored relocation plan with specific neighbourhood recommendations.

02

Pre-Arrival Preparation

School applications filed 90 days before arrival. Housing shortlisted and virtual tours arranged. Work permit and bank introduction letter prepared. Arrival logistics (airport meet, first-night accommodation) confirmed.

03

Arrival & Orientation

We meet you at Entebbe, transfer to Kampala and deliver your area orientation. Housing viewings are arranged within 48 hours of arrival. Preferred clients move into furnished temporary accommodation arranged by us.

04

Settlement & Registration

Bank account opened within one week. URA TIN registered. Healthcare providers introduced. Utilities and internet connected. Household staff presented and hired. School confirmed and first day arranged.

05

30-Day Check-In

We contact you one month after arrival to resolve any outstanding issues and connect you to the Kampala expat community, relevant social clubs and professional networks.
