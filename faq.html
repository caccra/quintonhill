01 — Business Registration

Registering a company
in Uganda

Uganda is one of East Africa's most active investment destinations. Registering a business is straightforward with the right guidance — here is what you need to know.

How long does company registration take in Uganda?

With professional assistance, company registration in Uganda through URSB typically takes 3 to 10 business days. Without local support, the process often takes 4 to 8 weeks due to documentation requirements, multiple visits to URSB offices, and procedural delays.

Quinton Hill Concierge handles URSB registration end-to-end, including document preparation, name reservation, submission, and follow-up — so you receive your certificate without setting foot in a government queue.

What documents are needed to register a business in Uganda?

Standard documentation required for company registration in Uganda includes:

  • Valid passport or national ID for all directors and shareholders
  • Proposed company name (with two alternatives in case the first is taken)
  • Memorandum and Articles of Association
  • Details of the registered office address in Uganda
  • Prescribed URSB registration fee

Additional requirements apply for sector-specific businesses (e.g., financial services, healthcare, NGOs) and for companies with foreign directors. Quinton Hill prepares all documentation on your behalf.

Can a foreigner register a company in Uganda?

Yes. Foreign nationals and foreign-owned companies can register businesses in Uganda. Foreign-owned companies may register as a subsidiary of a foreign company, a branch office, or as a locally incorporated company with foreign shareholding. Certain sectors require a minimum local ownership percentage or a special licence. Quinton Hill provides sector-specific guidance on the appropriate structure for your business.

02 — Regulatory Bodies

Understanding URSB
and URA

Two government bodies that every business in Uganda will interact with: URSB for registration, and URA for taxation.

What is URSB in Uganda?

URSB stands for the Uganda Registration Services Bureau. It is the government body responsible for company registration, business name registration, and intellectual property registration in Uganda. All companies operating in Uganda must be registered with URSB. URSB issues the Certificate of Incorporation upon successful registration, which is the foundational document proving a company's legal existence in Uganda.

URSB offices are located in Kampala. Online services are available for some applications, though in-person submission is still required for certain document types. Quinton Hill maintains established working relationships with URSB and manages submissions on behalf of clients.

What is URA registration in Uganda?

URA stands for the Uganda Revenue Authority — the national tax authority. All registered businesses in Uganda must register with URA to obtain a Tax Identification Number (TIN). The TIN is required to file taxes, apply for import and export licences, issue formal receipts, transact with government departments, and open certain business bank accounts.

URA registration typically follows URSB registration and is handled concurrently by Quinton Hill Concierge as part of the business registration package.

03 — Work Permits

Getting a work permit
in Uganda

Foreign nationals working in Uganda require a valid work permit. The process has specific documentation requirements and takes several weeks.

How do I get a work permit in Uganda?

Foreign nationals employed in Uganda require a Class G work permit. The application is submitted to the Directorate of Citizenship and Immigration Control. Required documents typically include:

  • Job offer letter from a Uganda-registered employer
  • Academic and professional certificates (notarised)
  • Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity)
  • Passport photographs
  • Police clearance certificate from the home country (within 6 months)
  • Prescribed government fee
  • Company documents: Certificate of Incorporation, Trading Licence, TIN

Processing typically takes 4 to 8 weeks. Quinton Hill facilitates the full application — from documentation preparation to submission and follow-up.

Note: Investors who own 51% or more of a Uganda-registered company may apply for an Investor's Class permit rather than an employment permit.
04 — Documentation

Police clearance certificates
in Uganda

A police clearance certificate is required for many official processes in Uganda, including work permits, visa applications, and certain professional registrations.

What is a police clearance certificate and how do I get one?

A police clearance certificate (also called an Interpol certificate) is an official document confirming an individual has no criminal record. It is issued by the Criminal Intelligence Directorate (CID) of the Uganda Police Force for Ugandan residents, or by the equivalent authority in the applicant's home country for foreign nationals.

For Uganda-based applications: the process involves submitting fingerprints, a copy of your passport or national ID, and the prescribed fee to the CID headquarters in Kampala. Processing takes 5 to 15 business days.

For foreign nationals who need a clearance from their home country: the certificate must typically be issued by the national police authority and apostilled. Quinton Hill advises on the specific requirements for your nationality.

05 — Relocation

Relocating to Kampala,
Uganda

Kampala is a growing city with a vibrant business community. Relocating here is exciting — and manageable with the right local support.

What are the steps to relocate to Kampala, Uganda?

A successful relocation to Kampala typically involves:

  1. Visa and immigration — Secure the appropriate visa or work permit before travel
  2. Accommodation — Find a suitable home in a neighbourhood that fits your lifestyle and security requirements
  3. Banking — Open a Ugandan bank account (requires passport, proof of address, and employer letter)
  4. School enrollment — For families with children: identify schools, arrange visits, and complete admissions
  5. Embassy registration — Register with your home country's embassy in Kampala
  6. Utilities and connectivity — Set up electricity, water, internet, and mobile services
  7. Local orientation — Understand transport, healthcare, shopping, and daily life logistics

Quinton Hill Concierge manages every step of this process for relocating executives, families, and corporate employees.

06 — Investment

Investment due diligence
in Uganda

Uganda's investment market offers genuine opportunity. It also carries specific risks that proper due diligence eliminates before capital is committed.

How does investment due diligence work for Uganda?

Before investing in a Ugandan business, property, or opportunity, independent due diligence should verify:

  • Company registration status with URSB — confirm the company is legally incorporated and in good standing
  • Director and shareholder identity — confirm who actually controls the business
  • Physical existence — site visits to verify operations are real
  • Financial standing — review of available financial records and bank references
  • Legal encumbrances — confirm no liens, lawsuits, or regulatory problems
  • Land title verification — for property investments, confirm title deed authenticity
  • Regulatory compliance — confirm licences, permits, and compliance status

Quinton Hill Concierge conducts all of the above independently, with no interest in the outcome — giving investors a factual basis for their decision.

Fraud risk: Uganda's informal investment market has a high incidence of misrepresentation. Companies presenting falsified URSB certificates, directors operating under aliases, and property sellers without valid title deeds are documented risks. Independent due diligence is essential, not optional.

08 — Travel

Visas, travel & consultation
in and out of Uganda

Whether you are entering Uganda or travelling from Uganda to another country, Quinton Hill's travel team handles documentation, applications, and full trip planning so the process is seamless.

How do I get a visa for Uganda?

Uganda offers several visa categories depending on purpose and duration of stay:

  • East African Tourist Visa — valid for Uganda, Kenya, and Rwanda. Apply online via the Uganda e-visa portal. Processing: 3–5 business days.
  • Single-entry tourist visa — for stays up to 90 days. Available online or on arrival at Entebbe International Airport.
  • Business visa — for business-related visits. Typically 30–90 days, extendable in-country.
  • Multiple-entry visa — for frequent travellers and regional business people. Valid 6–12 months.

Many nationalities receive visa-on-arrival. Others must apply in advance. Quinton Hill's travel team handles visa applications, tracks processing status, and coordinates arrival for corporate clients and relocating professionals.

E-visa portal: Uganda's online visa application is managed through the Immigration Department's official portal. Quinton Hill facilitates applications and monitors processing status for clients.

What is the Uganda visa application process step by step?

The standard process for obtaining a Uganda visa through the e-visa portal is:

  1. Determine your visa category — tourist, business, East African Tourist, or multiple-entry. Your nationality and purpose of travel determine which applies.
  2. Gather documents — valid passport (minimum 6 months validity, at least 2 blank pages), passport-size photographs, return flight itinerary, hotel or host confirmation, and proof of sufficient funds.
  3. Submit online application — complete the form on Uganda's official e-visa portal and upload scanned copies of all required documents.
  4. Pay the visa fee — fees vary by category: single-entry tourist USD 50; East African Tourist Visa USD 100; multiple-entry (6 months) USD 100; multiple-entry (12 months) USD 150. Payment is by credit/debit card online.
  5. Await approval — standard processing is 3–5 business days. Quinton Hill monitors your application and follows up with the immigration authority if required.
  6. Print your visa approval — once granted, print the approval notice and present it at the port of entry alongside your passport.
Quinton Hill handles this entire process for clients — document collection, form completion, submission, monitoring, and confirmation. You provide the documents; we handle everything else.

How far in advance should I apply for a visa when travelling abroad from Uganda?

Lead times vary significantly by destination. As a general guide:

  • UK, USA, Canada, Schengen — apply 8–12 weeks in advance. These embassies can have long appointment queues, particularly in Kampala.
  • UAE, India, China — apply 3–4 weeks in advance. Processing is generally faster but documentation requirements are strict.
  • East African countries (Kenya, Rwanda, Tanzania) — most allow visa-on-arrival or e-visa within 2–5 business days. Apply at least one week before travel.
  • South Africa — apply 4–6 weeks in advance. Biometric appointments are required at the South African High Commission in Kampala.

Quinton Hill advises starting the process as early as possible. We flag all deadlines and keep the process on track so you never miss a departure because of a documentation delay.
